Report on the two-day Open Retriever Trial from 14th to 15th September 2018

The Essex Field Trial Society opened their 2018-2019 season with the two-day Open Retriever Trial at Lilac Farm, Eastmoor, near Kings Lynn by kind permission of Mr. Will Chapman.

Judges were Mr. Phil Allan, Mr. Tim Brain, Mrs. Gaynor Gent and Mrs. Charlotte Jeffery.

Mr. Richard Bickerleg was the Steward of the Beat and, as always, provided us with splendid conditions which were matched with fair weather and good shooting by our faithful team of guns led by Bob Hales.

The trial was well supported with 118 entries and the full card of 24 started with a walked-up drive over sugar beet. We dropped a number of dogs in the first round and a duck drive for the second round concluded the day with 10 dogs remaining to go forward to the second day.

On Saturday, and once again blessed with good weather, the second round was swiftly concluded and the third round gave some fine work and handling, the usual few examples of hard luck, and the fourth round brought the trial to a time conclusion, with the first three dogs being very closely judged.

The result was:

  • 1st – Mrs. L. Hills’ Stauntonvale Tic Bean
  • 2nd – Mrs. Sharon Dingle’s Belspaddle Intrepid
  • 3rd – Mrs. Louie Robertson’s FtCh. Mitforton Macho

Certificates of Merit went to Ronnie Laughton’s Wytonruss Finnegan and also Mr. Wayne Mitchell’s Turpingreen Barrera of Labdom, who also received the trophy for the Gun’s Choice.

Warm thanks to all who assisted and contributed to making this such a successful and great trial.

Report on the Novice Retriever Trial on 8th January 2018

We returned to Lilac Farm, Eastmoor, near Kings Lynn for our third and final Retriever Trial of the 2017-2018 season. Mr. Will Chapman kindly invited us back to his grounds, and Richard Bickerleg, the Chief Steward, once again provided us with outstanding grounds and game. This Novice competition was well supported with almost 50 entries. Judges were Phil Parkins, Mike Skipper, Paul Truby and David Wall with Bob Hales once again providing a crack team of guns for the day.

We began with a walked-up drive through a copse, moved through to standing game crop and completed the first drive with a number of birds which dropped into rough, marshy ground on the far side of a wide ditch. This proved a bit challenging for some of the really novice dogs and a number went out. However, it tested obedience, game finding and retrieving skills and it became clear we had a very interesting competition on. We proceeded with another walked-up drive, and this was sufficient to complete the trial.

First prize and the C. J. Moorhouse trophy went to Henrik Vilendal’s Vilendal Quasimodo, and second prize was awarded to Barry Taylor’s Valleyend Arndilly in a very close finish. A Certificate of Merit and Gun’s Choice went to Keith Townsend’s Elmertown Special One. All three dogs were worthy of awards and the two runners-up will surely be winning soon.

Thank you to all the cheery helpers and fellow competitors who stayed for the prize-giving, which made for a celebratory feel to the close of our season. A thoroughly enjoyable day. We wish you all a good summer and look forward to meeting again for the 2018 season.

Happy New Year!
