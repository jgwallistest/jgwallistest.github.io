Projects

Private Gas Networks

Project Background

Breckland Utility Solutions Limited was awarded a contract to create mathematical network models of a number of networks owned by the MoD. Breckland Utility Solutions carried out detailed surveys of the sites alongside other contractors undertaking pipeline camera surveys, and created scaled asset record drawings for use as the basis of the network models. The next stage was to quantitatively verify that each gas network was substantially behaving as predicted by theoretical analysis.

New Technique

A new technique for validating the gas network models involved filming the various gas meters within each site’s network to be measured under known demand conditions and compared with that network’s theoretical behaviour.

Benefits

This technique enables a greater degree of accuracy of flow behaviour to be measured than can be achieved by meter pulse measurement and provides a full audit trail of the results of the pressure survey, whilst keepin costs to a minimum. Survey data was captured using tripod-mounted cameras capable of producing high-quality video output. The video output from each meter and pressure gauge was then synchronised and manually reviewed, producing simultaneous readings at regular intervals. This information was then logged on a spreadsheet and the results processed for direct comparison with the results predicted by the network model.
